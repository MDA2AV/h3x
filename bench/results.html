h2load dropped 0 requests in all 72 cells. The h2load collapse at m=1-2 happens only against h2o (7k-54k req/s there vs 480k-820k against nginx and 410k-540k against haproxy): a pairing-specific interaction between ngtcp2 and the quicly server at low concurrency, not general client behavior. Against nginx the picture inverts: no GSO-batched responses means h3x's GRO cannot engage, quicly pays full per-packet receive cost, and h2load leads everywhere above m=1 while h3x also develops a failure tail. Against haproxy the throughputs tie within noise (haproxy GSO-batches, so h3x's GRO engages again), but h3x drops requests in every cell, growing with both axes to 36,631 at 512x64, while h2load stays clean. Single unpinned runs: ratios under ~1.5x are within run-to-run noise.
